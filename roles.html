PLDI Student Volunteer Roles

Here is a complete list of student volunteer duties, by role. Note that you may be called on to do other things at the spur of the moment, so this should only serve as a rough guide. In general, your scheduled volunteer timeslot starts 15 minutes or more before the start of the event you need to work. If you happen to notice that this is not the case, I made a mistake, so please be sure to arrive early!

Bag Stuffer

  • Prepare bags for attendees. Basically, putting flyers, schedules, and conference goodies into tote bags / folders to be handed out to conference attendees who come to the registration desk.

Registrar / Info Desk

  • People will come to you to pick up their badges.
  • Find and give them their name tag.
  • Give them a conference bag prepared by the bag stuffers.
  • If their name tag is missing, if they have not completed registration (pink slip), or they have not registered, refer them to Carol, who will take care of this.
  • Familiarize yourself with the conference schedule and the locations of events and be prepared to direct people to the appropriate location.

WS (workshop) Assistant

  • Be sure to arrive before the start of the session.
  • Introduce yourself to the workshop session chair. Let him/her know that you are the SV assigned to help out and that you are there to help them solve problems.
  • IMPORTANT: make sure that the projector is present, set up, and turned on.
  • Ensure that the workshop session chair has all of the adapters that they need for presenters. If not, please see Dan, Michael, or Annabel at the registration desk. We can acquire adapters for you.
  • Likewise, if there are room temperature issues, see Dan, Michael, or Annabel.
  • IMPORTANT: Wait 15 minutes after the start of the session for the back-and-forth to settle down and then count the number of attendees. Post the counts in the #headcounts Slack channel.
  • If there is a microphone for the audience (check with the chair), make sure that you know how to use it and that it works.
  • Hang around the room in case there are issues.

Floating Tutorial Assistant

  • The responsibilities are the same as the WS Assistant, except:
  • You will need to cover several rooms.
  • There probably is not an audience microphone, since tutorials are smaller.

Lunch Duty

  • Confer with the other SV who is assigned to your slot because there are two main roles. You may decide which of you does which role.
  • Role #1: Ensure that only people with conference badges are attending the lunch. If they do not have a badge, politely ask them to please go get their badge and return.
  • Role #2: Lunch is buffet-style. Anyone who has a special request for vegetarian / vegan / gluten-free / etc meals will have a dot on their badge. Green = vegan / vegetarian. Red = gluten free / other. A small number of these special meals will be available at the buffet. Please station yourself near the table and check badges to ensure that only people who requested special meals take this food. If you see a person with no dot on their badge heading toward one of these special meals, politely direct them toward the other food. If they say that they forgot to request a special meal, that's fine (no need to fight about it), but be sure that they understand that there is a limited amount of special request food.

Video Recording

  • You are responsible for capturing the audio and video (including video of the presenter and of the slides) during the technical sessions / keynotes.
  • Please attend a training session with Bader Albassam (see contacts on main page).
  • You should start the recording when the session starts.
  • End the recording when the session ends.
  • Be sure to capture the Q&A portion of the talk if at all possible (see Bader). Be sure to introduce yourself to the volunteer Session Assistant, as they may be able to assist you (e.g., by requesting that questioners step up to the mic).
  • Ensure that the post-production person, David Darais, has access to the video (see Bader for instructions).

Session Assistant

  • The Session Assistant largely does the same job as the one performed by the WS Assistant, except:
  • Technical sessions are a bit more high-stress situations for session chairs and presenters. Please be sure to be very polite and helpful even though they may be distracted or rude. Be very prompt with help if they ask for it.
  • Technical sessions are being recorded by a team of video production volunteers. Be sure to introduce yourself to the SV doing that, and let them know that you can help if they need it.
  • Every technical talk is followed by a Q&A after the talk. Make sure that there is a microphone available for the audience to use, and that you know how to use it. We usually place the microphone in the center aisle. Keep it turned off during the talk, but turn it on immediately after. There is often a switch on the mic itself, but check with the video production person to be sure.
  • Make sure that questioners from the audience step up to the microphone when they ask questions.
  • REMEMBER to give us headcounts in the #headcounts Slack channel!
  • NOTE that the SIGPLAN Town Hall will not be video recorded. However, there may be a lengthy Q&A period. Please be sure to introduce yourself to the SIGPLAIN Chair, Michael Hicks, before the session.

Poster Setup

  • Volunteers will be setting up Student Research Competition (SRC) posters for presenters. It is very important that all setup be completed before the SRC judging begins!
  • You will need to obtain poster boards and pins. See Dan, Michael, or Annabel.

Poster Session Reception

  • Introduce yourself to the SRC poster presenters and SRC judge panel and let them know that you are available to assist them.
  • Make sure that SRC presenters have their name badges!

Banquet

  • Note that all SVs are welcome to attend the banquet! This means that you are welcome to mingle and eat food as long as it is not your shift to work. Make sure that you bring your ticket.
  • If you are working, please stand by the entrance and collect Banquet tickets from conference attendees. Note that not everybody has a ticket. They are only given out to people who pay the full price for the conference as well as SVs. Note that senior community members sometimes give their tickets to their students or the first authors on their papers. But people *must* have a ticket to be granted admission. Attendees are not welcome if they do not have a ticket; politely explain that a ticket is required.
  • We intentionally kept SV banquet slots short so that you could enjoy yourself. Please do!
  • Also: many SVs are first-year graduate students and do not know anybody. If you see an SV wandering around with a lost look in their eyes, please be welcoming and introduce them to your friends. Being welcoming and charitable with newcomers is one of the perks of being a volunteer, and we need your help to make sure that this happens!

Closing

  • Generally speaking, the people assigned to help with closing help us "put stuff away". This may involve packing things up into boxes, moving things around (like tables or chairs), and helping with odd jobs needed by Dan, Michael, Annabel, or Ben.
